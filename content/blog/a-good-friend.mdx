Today I want to talk about friendship.

I don't know if you've ever felt this way — I certainly have. After a long night of hanging out with friends, or exhausting ourselves together, I often feel a sudden emptiness.

If you live close to home, maybe it's not so bad. But when you're far from home, it's really hard to find someone to truly talk to. Someone to dump your mental clutter and garbage into.

Suddenly I miss those days of arguing with family, or with the deskmate I sat next to for three years. I miss that kind of release.

It feels like we've been smiling all the time since we grew up. No time for sadness. No time for tears. Or maybe we have the time, have the emotions — but no one to seriously listen.

A couple of days ago, I saw an old classmate's post on Moments:

"部分可见. Late night negativity explosion. I caught someone who happened to reply to me (a close acquaintance, or so I thought) and said: let me vent. Sent a bunch of stickers. They said: I'm not your guy best friend. If you keep this up I'm deleting you. I froze. Withdrew everything I'd said. The tears just came. No one is obligated to be nice to you. No one is obligated to listen to your complaints and your temper."

There was more after that — the usual keep-living-well stuff. I didn't screenshot the rest.

Even though I was in the "部分可见" list, if she'd come to me, I might not have listened properly either. I'd want to study my vocabulary. Watch my movie. Just like she said — no one is obligated.

If she'd come to me, I'd probably say I was busy first.

We might be acquaintances. But not friends.

And even if I listened, I wouldn't know what's going on around her lately. What she's going through right now. I wouldn't know how to comfort her any better than the "less close" people around her probably could.

At times like this, I think everyone feels the same. They wish they had a really, really good friend. Not as anxious as family. Not as blind as a lover. Not as solitary as yourself.

A few days ago I was talking to a friend. He told me he'd recently made a friend he could open up to. But he was troubled by it.

I smirked. "It's not a girl, is it? You have a girlfriend, don't mess around."

He was silent for a long time. Didn't answer directly. He told me the story instead.

He and this girl were in the same department. They'd chat and laugh occasionally when they ran into each other. One time he felt she seemed off — carried some heavy thoughts — and casually asked. She said she'd broken up with her boyfriend.

But what followed wasn't some cheesy drama. My friend, to avoid any misunderstanding, didn't pry. Just offered some comfort.

Time passed. Not long after, they ended up in the same group for a social activity. They gradually got closer. He told me she wasn't what he'd imagined. He'd assumed she was the type to party every day and live wild. But she was actually driven, and remarkably unpretentious.

He said he found it fascinating — someone so fundamentally different from him. She was serious about her work. Polite. Managed her time well. He went on for a while.

I laughed. "You're in heat, aren't you? She's your goddess now?"

He replied with three words: "It's different." Told me to shut up.

After they got back, they talked a lot. Mostly about life and family. About friendship. He said for the first time in years, he'd poured his heart out to someone.

But then, when the initial rush faded and the intensity gradually subsided, there came the silence and occasional awkwardness that follows getting to know someone. He said this was more troubling than the awkwardness of being strangers.

I didn't know what to say. All the chicken soup I'd read online turned into footwash water in the face of this reality. I thought about it for a long time and didn't reply.

That's where the story ends. Our conversation moved on to recent developments and current situations. And so it fizzled out.

Afterward, I was quietly glad he'd shared this story with me. And I酝酿ed to write it down.

On the third night after our chat, I received a message from him:

"You never know if you don't try. Precious things don't come easily."

At that moment, staring at the screen — that girl, I don't know you. But I see you.

Friend. Good night.
