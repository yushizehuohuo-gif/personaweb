It started as a curiosity. Can AI capture the essence of a Dota 2 hero in a radically different proportion?

The answer turned out to be more nuanced than yes or no.

## The Proportion Problem

Chibi (Q版) art follows specific rules: approximately 2-3 head heights, rounded features, oversized heads, simplified limbs. Most AI models are trained predominantly on standard anime proportions. Ask for "chibi" and you'll often get something in between — neither full anime nor true chibi.

## The Solution: Start from Zero

After dozens of failed img2img attempts, I discovered the counter-intuitive truth: for true chibi proportions, you need to generate from scratch. img2img with low denoising constrains the output to the original body structure, preventing the round, compact silhouette that defines Q版.

The approach that worked: describe the character's visual identity in detail (species, colors, signature items), then prompt for chibi directly. The model needs freedom to restructure proportions.

## 200 Heroes Later

Each hero presented unique challenges. Some have complex armor that doesn't simplify well. Others rely on proportion cues (like Pudge's massive frame) that disappear in chibi scale. The creative challenge became: what's the minimum information needed to make a hero recognizable at miniature scale?

That, I think, is the essence of good character design.
