This website you're looking at right now is my latest experiment in visual identity.

## The Brief

I gave myself a constraint: design a personal site that feels like a magazine cover, not a resume. No dashboards. No skill bars. No "I'm a developer who loves clean code" energy.

Instead: bold typography, high contrast, cherry red accents against warm dark backgrounds. Editorial composition where every screen is a deliberate layout, not a template.

## The References

Fashion campaign posters. Album covers. Creative studio websites. Japanese editorial design. The kind of visual language where text is treated as a graphic element, not a container for information.

Space Grotesk became the typographic anchor — geometric, confident, with just enough personality without becoming decorative. The color palette reduced to four: Cherry Red, Cyber Pink, Aqua Mint, Warm Orange. Nothing else needed.

## Less Information, More Impact

The hardest edit was removing things. Every traditional portfolio instinct says "show more work, write more about yourself, add more pages." The creative director instinct says "make them feel something first, explain later."

That's the philosophy I'm bringing to every project now. Visual emotion over information density. Always.
