## The Shift

Something changed when I first connected ComfyUI to my creative pipeline. Suddenly, the gap between what I imagined and what I could produce narrowed dramatically.

As an independent creator working across game design and visual art, I've always been constrained by my own hands. I can design systems, compose scenes, and direct aesthetics — but painting every asset, rendering every character variation, iterating on every visual direction? That's a team's worth of work.

AI doesn't replace the creative direction. It amplifies it.

## Finding the Voice

The hardest part isn't generating images. It's maintaining a consistent visual identity across dozens of outputs. My Q版 chibi series taught me that prompt engineering alone isn't enough — you need to build workflows that encode your aesthetic decisions.

Every LoRA, every ControlNet setup, every ComfyUI node graph is a crystallized creative decision. That's where the art lives.

## What's Next

I'm exploring ways to bring AI-assisted workflows into Godot, building tools that let me iterate on game assets in real-time. The goal isn't automation — it's acceleration. Faster iteration means more time for the decisions that actually matter.
