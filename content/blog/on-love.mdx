Hey. I should really be sleeping.

I remember it well. I was thirteen when I fell in love for the first time.

Don't laugh at me. Love back then was always so simple. But even so, it overflowed with shy sentiments. We'd meet and couldn't say a word. Yet we both burned with the desire to be together. Just a few more glances at each other — that was enough to fill our hearts.

"A day without you feels like three autumns." But I didn't even know what I missed about her. Her beauty? Probably. Her tenderness? Passable. Her sweet smile? But she didn't smile at me that often.

Maybe none of that. Maybe it was just that quiet tenderness in my heart — and only she could fill that space.

Later, when that first love faded, I fell in love many more times. But that initial racing heart — that most ambiguous expression, that most restrained shyness, that most狂热的 soul — it all drifted away from us.

So we always blazed bright and then burned out, and when the song ended, we'd honestly say three words: "Let's break up."

"Should've said that a long time ago..."

Maybe because we were young, we treated feelings carelessly. Like toys. Play with them. If it's fun, keep going. If not, throw them away.

A few more years passed. When our thoughts and feelings had evolved past the clamor, we'd sit quietly at home singing *Later*, realizing how many loves we'd let slip by. Regretting.

We might think the most precious thing in the world has been lost. Eventually we turn to Buddhism. Go with the flow. Follow fate. Never again force an illusory love. Convince ourselves that loving a few times in a lifetime is enough.

But maybe that's not the truth of it. Maybe we're still young. Can't handle the bitterness of love without the sweetness.

Time flows like water. Looks still — but it's always moving. When youth becomes a thing of the past, we put on a brave face, saying it's no big deal, there'll be someone better. Carry this daze into searching for the next. But we always find the same incompleteness.

Eventually we stop believing in true love.

Then the age comes. We marry whoever's available. Bury those unfeeling glances, sweet and tender words, colorful midnights, poetic and romantic rainy seasons... all suppressed or stored away in some beautiful corner of memory.

When a heavy snow covers all of this — our feelings, our love, our desires all buried deep — all that's left is a love story in the falling snow. And one sincere realization:

Cherish what you have now.

Good night.
