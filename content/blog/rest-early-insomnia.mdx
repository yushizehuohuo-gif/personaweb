---
title: "Rest Early, Insomnia"
date: "2026-06-09"
excerpt: "A late-night reflection on goodnights, white bears, and the strange intimacy of saying 'rest early' to people who exist in the gaps between day and night."
tags: ["Night", "Memory"]
---

It was near midnight tonight. I picked up my phone, meaning to tell a few close ones to rest early and good night. Then my heart tightened.

Because it seemed like just a way of saying goodbye for the evening. Nobody really cares whether you actually sleep early. And for a moment, I wasn't even sure if they were truly close to me.

I put the phone down. Like taking off a mask.

Then, out of nowhere, I remembered the big white bear I used to hug to sleep when I was little. I vaguely recall its paws were made of leather. The rest was white and soft.

No matter who I said goodnight to, it was always my mother who came in to tuck me in, or turn the AC up two degrees. Then the light between the door and the frame narrowed, narrower... and suddenly opened again. "Rest early, good night."

When I got older, I'd stay up late talking to that girl. The white bear wasn't so big anymore. It sat beside me all night, facing the flickering TV screen. Silent. The light playing on its white fur.

No matter who I said goodnight to, it was always my mother who came in to tuck me in, or turn the AC up two degrees. Then the light between the door and the frame narrowed, narrower... and suddenly opened again. "Rest early, stop playing on your phone, good night."

The seasons passed. Time yellowed the bear's fur, wore out the leather on its paws, and sent me far away.

Now I live with three other guys my age. We fall asleep one by one. Different snoring sounds. But I hardly ever rest early anymore. Every night, lying in bed, I think more than I do all day. Deeper thoughts.

Gradually I understand why believers choose to pray late at night. The soul is especially still then.

Many times, after saying goodnight, I start another nightlife unrelated to those people. Or I just lie in the dark thinking.

Close your eyes. Don't look at that dazzling world. Too tiring.

Try to sleep. Maybe because the day wasn't tiring enough. Maybe because the heart isn't still enough. Or maybe because I've loaded too many burdens I shouldn't carry, too many intersections of souls.

I toss and turn. Eventually I pick up my phone and say to one or two familiar ones: "Rest early, good night."

Then I think twice. Put it down again. Start over. Again and again. Finally, insomnia.

Until I finally take off the mask I've been wearing all day.

Rest early. Good night. Bye.
