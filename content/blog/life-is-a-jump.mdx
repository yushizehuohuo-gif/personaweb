I don't know where to begin. People always start with this line. Maybe because nothing really has a beginning or an end.

I spent several nights standing on the rooftop imagining what it would feel like to jump. Imagining the sharp pain at the moment of impact. Then I started to doubt myself. Doubt that I'm mentally strong enough — I'd probably have passed out from fear before I even hit the ground.

Would I enjoy the sensory rush of the rapid descent? Would I even find pleasure in it?

I feel fear. Every time I imagine making the decision to jump, what brings fear isn't the act itself, or the process. What brings fear is knowing that you might regret it in that last second — and be powerless to do anything about it. Regret the version of yourself who jumped. Be afraid of no longer being able to think.

If the building were taller, I'd also have to face the intense stimulation of free fall. The visual rush.

Jump? Jump. Jump? Don't jump.

Can I fucking not jump?

You think your life can just say "I don't want to start" and it won't start? Say "stop, I want to go back to the rooftop and think a bit more" — and you think you won't get old and sick and die?

Wake up, darling. You're going to die. You're going to die.

Every extra day you live brings you one day closer to death. Scared, huh?

Smile. Don't sit there with your phone, feeling a shiver you'll forget in two days.

Life — maybe it's crueler than jumping off a building. Because you can't even choose. This isn't a coward's game. The sad — even pathetic — thing is that the person typing these words might be sitting there cracking sunflower seeds, staring at a computer screen, playing word games to fool the world. Maybe he himself is a clown who closes his eyes tight when jumping, not even brave enough to scream.

But maybe you're different from me. Maybe you have a lighter in your pocket. Set yourself on fire and fall like a shooting star. You can roar and howl all the way down. You can grab hold of someone falling beside you, feel the warmth of their fingertips.

You can land face-up, so at least you'll have a whole face when you die.

In the end we all hit the ground. Turn into smashed flesh and blood paste. Melt into the soil. White, hard bones pile up high, sending off the next one to fly.

Oh. No. To fall.

And then die.
